Exercise for Children

Exercise for children does not mean running on a treadmill or lifting weights at the gym. Exercise simply means being active and moving around!. Children who are physically fit have stronger bones and muscles, have better sleeping patterns, and have a decreased risk of developing type II diabetes.

Tips

  1. Get moving everyday! Start out with 20 minutes and gradually increase the amount of time. Exercising for an hour a day is the goal!
  2. Be a good example! If your children see you being active, they are more likely to do the same.
  3. Even if you can’t go outside, move around indoors! Do jumping jacks or dance around to your child’s favorite song!
  4. Exercising is more fun with friends! Have your child invite friends over to play tag or start a soccer game.
  5. Keep a chart on the refrigerator to track your child's daily physical activity.
